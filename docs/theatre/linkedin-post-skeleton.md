## Candidate framing

### Frame A (€-led)

A €5M-spend tenant with 100+ active vendors loses 2-4% of vendor spend per year to unmanaged risk. That's €100K-€200K/year in preventable leakage. The signals to catch it exist — they just live in three different systems and nobody is correlating them.

I built a vendor performance intelligence engine for that gap. Composite risk scoring across four signal sources, rolling 30/90/180-day windows, band-crossing alerts to a notification hub, PDF scorecards that re-render byte-identically 30 days later for the audit committee.

The hardest part wasn't the scoring. It was making sure the audit log of every alert and every report was legally defensible — a tenant rename mid-flight can't poison the historical record.

### Frame B (process-led)

Most vendor scorecards I've seen at mid-market manufacturers are assembled manually each quarter by an analyst who pulls data from three or four systems and calls vendors with red flags. The work takes a week. The data is six months old by the time it lands.

Continuous vendor scoring with explainable contributors changes that loop. The CPO sees, today, which 20 of 300 vendors are degrading and exactly which signals caused the band change. The quarterly scorecard becomes a re-render of the live state, not a one-shot artifact.
